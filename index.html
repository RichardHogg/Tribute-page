Life of Jimi

  • Born in Seattle, Washington, Hendrix began playing guitar at the age of 15.
  • In mid-1958, at age 15, Hendrix acquired his first acoustic guitar, for $5.
  • Before Hendrix was 19 years old he enlisted in the army on May 31, 1961.
  • After being granted an honorable discharge he left Tennessee and later moved to england.
  • In 1966 Hendrix had earned three UK top ten hits with the Jimi Hendrix Experience: "Hey Joe", "Purple Haze", and "The Wind Cries Mary".
  • 1968 his third and final studio album, Electric Ladyland, reached number one in the U.S.
  • The world's highest-paid performer, he headlined the Woodstock Festival in 1969 and the Isle of Wight Festival in 1970.
  • on September 18, 1970 at the age of 27 he died an accidental death from barbiturate-related asphyxia.

Rolling Stone ranked the band's three studio albums, Are You Experienced, Bold as Love and Electric Ladyland, among the 100 greatest albums of all time, and they ranked Hendrix as the greatest guitarist and the sixth greatest artist of all time.
